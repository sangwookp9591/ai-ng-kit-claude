# /aing office-hours — YC Office Hours

## The Six Questions

Answer these before building anything:

### 1. What problem are you solving?
- State it in one sentence from the user's perspective
- "When I [situation], I struggle with [pain point] because [root cause]"
- If you can't state the problem clearly, you don't understand it yet

### 2. Who has this problem?
- Specific persona, not "everyone"
- How many people? How often do they hit this problem?
- Are they willing to pay? How much?
- Can you reach them?

### 3. How do they solve it today?
- The status quo is ALWAYS your competition
- What's the current workaround? How painful is it?
- "They don't do anything" is a red flag — maybe it's not a real problem

### 4. Why is your solution better?
- Not "different" — better. Measurably.
- 10x better on one dimension beats 2x better on five
- What's the one thing you do that nobody else can?

### 5. Why now?
- What changed that makes this possible/necessary today?
- New technology? New regulation? New behavior?
- "It's always been a problem" means the timing isn't special

### 6. What's the smallest thing you can build to test this?
- Not MVP — MLP (Minimum Lovable Product)
- What can you ship in 1 week that proves demand?
- How will you measure success?

## Running Office Hours

1. **Ask** the six questions one at a time
2. **Challenge** every answer — "Are you sure? What evidence?"
3. **Reframe** vague answers into concrete claims
4. **Prioritize** — rank the biggest risks
5. **Decide** — build, pivot, or kill

## Output Format

```
## Office Hours Summary

**Problem:** [one sentence]
**Who:** [specific persona + TAM]
**Status quo:** [current solution]
**Why better:** [10x advantage]
**Why now:** [timing catalyst]
**First test:** [1-week experiment]

**Verdict:** BUILD / PIVOT / KILL
**Biggest risk:** [what could kill this]
**Next step:** [one specific action]
```
